Archiving/Dearchiving Utilities

Best

Zarchiver

Without ads, this app extracts 7z (7zip), zip, rar, rar5, bzip2, gz, XZ, iso, tar, arj, cab, lzh, lha, lzma, xar, tgz, tbz, Z, deb, rpm, zipx, mtz, chm, dmg, cpio, cramfs, img (fat, ntfs, ubf), wim, ecm, lzip, zst (zstd), egg, and alz files. It also lets you create 7z, zip, bz2, gz, XZ, lz4, tar, and zst (zstd) archives.

Runner-Up

RAR

Although it has adverstisements in the app, RAR lets you extract RAR, ZIP, TAR, GZ, BZ2, XZ, 7z, ISO, ARJ archives. It also lets you create RAR and ZIP archives

Video playback

Best

VLC for Android

This app lets you play back MKV, MP4, AVI, MOV, Ogg, FLAC, TS, M2TS, Wv and AAC files.

URL Shorteners

1. TinyURL

The TinyURL website is not mobile optimized. However, using the form below, you can create a TinyURL right from my website. The main standout feature is the ability to create a custom URL without signing up for an account.

Enter a long URL to make tiny:
Custom alias (optional):
https://tinyurl.com/
May contain letters, numbers, and dashes.

2. Rebrandly

Rebrandly has mobile optimized websites at rebrandly.com and rb.gy. They also have a mobile app for Android and iOS. Rebrandly's interface is strikingly similar to Bitly's. However, they allow you to track the number of clicks without signing up for an account(to use this, add a + to the end of your shortened link).

3. Bitly

Bitly's website at bitly.com is mobile optimized. They also have apps for Android and iOS. The only reason I would recommend Bitly over Rebrandly is recognition. When you send people a bit.ly link, they're more likely to trust it over an rb.gy link
