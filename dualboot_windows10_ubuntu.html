How to dual Boot Windows and Ubuntu/Lubuntu

Do I have the right supplies

To dual boot or live boot Linux with Windows, you will need the following things:

  • A Computer with Windows 7 or later/MacOS 10.1 or later/Linux(to write the USB drive)
  • A Target Computer running Windows(can be the same one you used to write the USB drive)
  • A drive with 4-8 GB memory(you can try one with more memory
  • Internet that your target computer can access

What type of OS should I Download

For most recent computers(that aren't running super slow) I would recommend Ubuntu. For a 32 bit computer and/or a computer running Windows Vista or earlier, I would recommend Lubuntu. Next, check your computer. If your computer is 32 bit, you need to download the 32 bit version of the OS. If you're not sure, download the 32 bit version.

Booting from the USB drive

After you write the drive, safely eject the usb drive(it will be under a boot partition), and plug it into your target computer(if your target computer is the one you're already on, you don't need to do anything).

For Windows 7 and earlier

First, look up the boot menu key for your computer

Make sure the target computer is off(if it isn't shut it down). Plug in your USB drive and a usb keyboard and mouse.

Turn on your computer. When you see the manufacturer logo, press your boot menu key. You should see a menu come up.

Press the down arrow on your keyboard until your USB drive is highlighted. Press Enter

You should see a menu. Press Enter or wait until it disappears. From here, you can test out Ubuntu/Lubuntu and install it alongside Windows.

For Windows 8/8.1

Press the Windows key and the I key on your keyboard simultaneously.

Click Update and Recovery, then click Recovery.

Click the Restart Now button under Advanced Startup

At the "Choose an option" screen , click "Use a device, then click "EFI USB Device"

You should see a menu. Press Enter or wait until it disappears. From here, you can test out Ubuntu/Lubuntu and install it alongside Windows.

For Windows 10

Press the Windows key and the I key on your keyboard simultaneously.

Click Update and security, then click Recovery

Click the Restart Now button under Advanced Startup

At "Choose an option", click "Use a device, then click "EFI USB Device"

You should see a menu. Press Enter or wait until it disappears. From here, you can test out Ubuntu/Lubuntu and install it alongside Windows.
